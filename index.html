Summary:

  • Consumption of organic matter at depth consumes oxygen
  • Mixing processes like eddies or salty bottom currents add oxygen

Perspective:

  • The deepest parts of the Baltic Sea have been poor in oxygen before industrialisation. But eutrophication has lead to oxygen free zones.
  • Our newest research indicates that the status is still deegrading, with increasing (shallower, thicker) deoxygenated zones.
